Reading Lines in the Formula List

The core concept of the WAVES Toolbar is the entering and displaying of equations by breaking them into structural chunks called lines. For example, in an equation like:

x2 + 2x + 1 = 0

one line would be the overall structure of the equation (i.e., [exponential expression] + 2x + 1 = 0). Since the exponent requires additional clarification to remove ambiguity, it is separated into a second line (superscript, with a [base] term and a [superscript] term). Both the base and superscript are additional lines, defining specific parts of the exponent. The equation as it appears in the software's interface is:

  • {000} <START> {001} + 2x +1 = 0
  • {001} <superscript> {002}{003}
  • {002} <base> x
  • {003} <super> 2

where each line is identified by number (i.e., {001}, {002}, etc.).

Although reading this series of lines may seem initially complex, it will soon become second nature, and will eliminate any ambiguities or miscommunications when reading or creating math constructs.
